ProjectHub is a visibility-and-exposure task, not a single-endpoint bug hunt.
Ordinary participants can cross organisational boundaries through core reads,
delegated-access state, operational surfaces, and crafted-input paths, while
non-privileged views also expose privileged metadata through inconsistent shaping.

Test-authoring is the right shape because the risk is fully observable at the HTTP
boundary. The suite has to enforce the dual contract: catch cross-boundary access,
secondary-path redaction failures, delegated-scope drift, unsafe input handling,
predictable tokens, and verbose errors without overblocking legitimate same-team flows.

Rubric decomposition: 5 critical merged chains, 8 major supporting items,
1 minor repeatability item, and 1 nitpick quality item.
Severity arithmetic: 5x5 + 8x3 + 1x2 + 1x1 = 52.

The critical items require multiple actors, state transitions, endpoint comparisons,
and body-level assertions. The supporting items cover mutation readback, special
visibility modes, role-scoped boundaries, positive controls, repeatability, and
sentinel-based test quality.
